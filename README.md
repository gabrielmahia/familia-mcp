## Why This Exists

> *"Families in Africa often function like corporations. This layer is massively overlooked."*  
> — AI thesis for East Africa, 2026

African families manage inheritance across borders, decades, and generations. The infrastructure to do this well — wills, trusts, caretaker records, diaspora property management — historically required expensive lawyers or was simply absent.

**1st world equivalent:** LegalZoom Estate Planning / Trust & Will / Fidelity Estate Services

## Tools (6)

| Tool | Function |
|------|----------|
| `succession_law_guide` | Kenya inheritance rights, intestacy rules, gender equity under Law of Succession Act 1972 |
| `will_writing_guide` | Requirements, costs, and checklist for writing a legal will in Kenya |
| `diaspora_property_guide` | POA, title verification, tax, remittance for Kenya diaspora managing property abroad |
| `inheritance_dispute_guide` | Resolution paths: family mediation, High Court probate, Land Disputes Tribunal |
| `caretaker_records_guide` | Setting up caretaker arrangements for elderly parents — agreements, payment, legal authority |
| `trust_structures_kenya` | Trust structures available in Kenya: discretionary, family, unit trust, chama |
